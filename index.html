About

Hi, I'm Grisella! I’m an instructional designer with a passion for creating learning experiences that are engaging, beautiful, and meaningful. I bring 9 years of experience in education and 4 years as an eLearning developer, starting my career as a dual-language elementary teacher before discovering my love for instructional design.

While teaching, I wore many hats, including curriculum writer and instructional technology specialist, where I created digital training materials and led professional development sessions for educators. That’s when I realized how much I enjoyed designing learning experiences that empower others.

Today, I specialize in eLearning development and LMS management (particularly Canvas), with a strong focus on user experience and visual design. I'm an Adobe Certified Professional in Captivate and proficient in tools like Adobe Illustrator, Audition, Photoshop, and InDesign. Whether I’m building interactive courses, managing projects in Asana, or collaborating across teams via Slack, I bring creativity, initiative, and a deep respect for the learning process to everything I do.

Currently, I work in the nonprofit education space supporting early childhood programs, and I’m constantly sharpening my skills through platforms like Udemy, LinkedIn Learning, and Coursera. I’m a lifelong learner at heart, and that curiosity fuels every project I take on.

Outside of work, you’ll find me reading (I started a moms book club!), picking up a new craft, and spending quality time with husband and our two young children.

Work

Mastering the Art of Beertending

For Baillies Brewing Company, great service is just as important as great beer. As a new brewery in a competitive craft beer market, first impressions matter. This interactive eLearning course prepares new beertenders to confidently welcome customers, explain beer styles, and make personalized recommendations.

 

Design Process

Initial Design

The initial design of this project started as a rough outline, which I created in a Word document. Since this course didn’t include any branching scenarios, the flow was straightforward. The outline helped map out the learning objectives, key interactions, and overall structure before building the course.

View Outline

Storyboard

After drafting a rough outline, I created a storyboard detailing each slide of the eLearning course, including voice-over scripts, navigation guidance, and development notes.

View Storyboard

 

To develop this course, I used Adobe Captivate Classic. I sourced stock images and used ChatGPT to generate images of the beertender and mock-ups of the six beers, which I edited in Adobe Illustrator. For example, adding the Baillies logo to the beertender’s apron. For voice-over, I used Eleven Labs and edited the audio in Adobe Audition.

To build a custom navigation menu, I followed Paul Wilson’s YouTube tutorial, How to Build Your Own Table of Contents in Adobe Captivate, and his flip card interaction tutorial to create the flip cards in the Creating Great Guest Experiences slide.

I planned to include closed captions throughout the project but ran into a challenge on the Beer Styles 101 slide. The six audio clips were triggered by advanced actions and didn’t appear on the Timeline, which prevented standard captioning. I resolved this by using JavaScript to enable closed captions and display text manually.

Canvas Course: Developmentally Appropriate Tech Use in Pre-K

In my current role as an eLearning Developer in the nonprofit early education space, I design engaging, accessible training for PK3 and PK4 teachers and instructional leaders. When I joined AppleTree, course content was often hidden behind links that opened in new windows, creating confusion and extra steps for users. To improve the learning experience, I used my HTML and Captivate skills to embed interactive modules and videos directly into Canvas, ensuring a seamless, click-to-learn experience.

This course, Developmentally Appropriate Use of Technology in Pre-K, features a custom landing page, interactive popups, knowledge checks, and a drag-and-drop activity, all displayed directly within Canvas. I collaborated with an instructional designer and used tools like Adobe Captivate Classic, Adobe Audition, Adobe Illustrator, and Eleven Labs AI voiceover. I also applied HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to optimize the experience across devices.

I’m constantly sharpening my skills by taking online courses, learning from experts in the eLearning field, and competing in challenges, like the Adobe Captivate Timer Challenge, where I placed 2nd and earned a free certification course. I'm now an Adobe Certified Professional in Captivate and passionate about creating content that’s not only functional, but delightful to use.
